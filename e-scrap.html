E-Scrap

Don’t throw it away and have it end up in the landfill!

What is Electronic Scrap (E-Scrap)

  • Mercury is in flat screens
  • Lead is in the CRT monitor
  • Cadmium is in the laptop batteries
  • Digital cameras
  • Fax machines and copiers
  • Pagers, calculators, and video games

As technology advances, more and more electronics become “obsolete”. But there is a way to give this equipment a second use and help protect the environment from the toxic materials so they may contain. It is not waste – it is a resource.

Is it harmful?

Electronics contain materials that contaminate water and soil and harm human and animal health.

  • Computers, processors, monitors, keyboards, networking systems.
  • Printers and scanners
  • Phones and cellphones
  • Digital cameras
  • Fax machines and copiers
  • Pagers, calculators, and video games

Our Solution

What can I do with my E-Scrap?

The CACRC is a R2 RIOS CERTIFIED nonprofit designed to handle this problem. We give your old electronics a second chance in schools, other nonprofits, and in the community. We recycle over 1,500 tons of e-scrap annually.

How the CACRC collects E-Scrap

  • Scheduled Community drop-off days
  • State wide pick-up from businesses, industries, and agencies.
  • Drop off at our warehouse for individuals and businesses between 8:30am & 4:00pm

General Qualifications For Donations:

Pick Up

Organizations can qualify for pickup by trained CACRC staff if they have:

  • Items should be in one location for pickup.
  • 50 items or more which can include individual printers, desktop computers, flat screen monitors, etc.(A box of wires or cables does not count as part of the 50 items, although they can be donated as well.)

Schedule dates are dependent on availability.

RECYCLING FEES:

The CACRC charges a $5 recycling fee per printer, scanner and fax machine. All other items are free to donate. We DO NOT accept CRT monitors or televisions. (See "What We Recycle")

PICK UP FEES:

The charges vary dependent on the pickup location. Call (225) 379-3577 for more information.

What We Recycle

What We Accept

  • Computer Hardware
  • Computer Accessories (keyboards, mice, speakers, etc.)
  • Laptops
  • Telephones / Telephone Systems
  • Security Systems
  • Cell Phones
  • DVD Movies & Video Games
  • MP3 Players
  • Playstations, Xboxes, Wii
  • Digital Cameras & Digital Video Recorders (DVRs)
  • Digital Picture Frames
  • Stereos > Networking Equipment
  • Circuit Boards (of any type)
  • Processors
  • Flat Screen Monitors
  • Telephone and Computer Cables
  • Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS)
  • Toner Cartridges
  • Ink Jet Cartridges
  • Portable Navigation & GPS Devices (Garmin, Magellan, Tom-Tom)
  • Fax Machines ($10 FEE)
  • Standard Printers \ Scanners ($10 FEE)
  • Large Printers ($25 FEE)

What We Do Not Accept

  • Appliances / Furniture
  • Copiers
  • CRTs
  • Televisions
  • CD’s / Media
  • Light Bulbs
  • Fluorescent Light Bulbs of any kind
  • Household Batteries

Quality, Environmental, Health and Safety Policy

  • CACRC is committed to electronic scrap reduction by providing recycled computers and equipment to schools, nonprofits, and low income families; and using the framework of reuse, recover and recycle for the material that flows through our facility.
  • CACRC is committed to customer satisfaction.
  • CACRC is committed to the prevention of pollution and prevention of injury and ill health.
  • CACRC is committed to continuous improvement.
  • CACRC is committed to complying with all legal and other stakeholders requirements relative to the quality, environmental and health and safety aspects of our business and all other QEH&S commimitments made.
  • CACRC is committed to providing a framework for setting and reviewing quality, environmental, health, data security, and safety goals.
  • CACRC is committed to manage used and end of life electronic equipment based on a reuse, recover, dispose hierarchy including onsite and downstream materials management throughout the recycling chain.
  • CACRC is committed to manage Focus Materials throughout the recycling chain to final disposition with due diligence to protect our environment and health and safety.
  • CACRC is committed to communicating and reinforcing this policy throughout our company and those working on our behalf, as well as, to our customers, our suppliers and to the public.
